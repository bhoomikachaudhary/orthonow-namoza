5 Signs Your Knee Pain Needs a Specialist

8 min read · OrthoNow Editorial

Knee pain is one of the most common reasons people put off seeing a doctor — it's easy to assume it'll resolve on its own with rest. Sometimes that's true. Sometimes it isn't, and waiting too long can turn a manageable issue into a more serious one.

1. The pain lasts more than two weeks

Minor strains usually improve within a few days. If discomfort persists beyond two weeks despite rest and over-the-counter pain relief, it's worth getting it looked at properly rather than continuing to self-manage.

2. You hear or feel clicking, locking, or grinding

Occasional clicking without pain is usually harmless. Clicking combined with pain, or a sensation that the knee is catching or locking mid-movement, often points to something mechanical — a meniscus tear or cartilage issue — that benefits from an early specialist assessment.

3. Swelling that doesn't go down

Swelling right after an injury is expected. Swelling that persists for several days, or that returns repeatedly after activity, can indicate ongoing joint inflammation that's worth investigating.

4. The knee feels unstable

A sense that the knee might "give way," especially on stairs or uneven ground, is one of the more important signs not to ignore — it often suggests ligament involvement.

5. It's affecting how you walk

If you've noticed yourself favoring one leg, limping, or changing your gait without thinking about it, that compensation pattern can create secondary issues elsewhere — hips and lower back especially — the longer it continues.

When to book a consultation

If two or more of these apply to you, it's a reasonable point to get a specialist opinion rather than waiting further. Early assessment generally means simpler treatment options.
